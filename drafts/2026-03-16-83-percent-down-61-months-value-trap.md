ARKG is down 83% from its peak. The drawdown has lasted 61 consecutive months. The sector it tracks has delivered genuine scientific breakthroughs in that same period.

This is what an unbroken value trap looks like from the inside. The narrative was never wrong -- genomics innovation is real, the milestones are verifiable, the science is advancing. What was wrong was the investment structure capturing it. Capital tied up for five years in negative compounding, in an ETF concentrated in pre-revenue biotechs with sustained FCF burn and no visible near-term catalyst for profitability.

The annualised return since inception sits at 4.31%, per lazyportfolioetf.com data as of February 2026. For context, that is what happens when an 83% drawdown compounds over years -- even a partially recovering price cannot undo the damage to time-weighted returns.

The diagnostic question the EdenFinTech system would apply here: is the drawdown a temporary narrative discount, or structural impairment? The FCF burn rates across the holdings, the absence of a visible catalyst for profitability, and the concentration in pre-revenue businesses would trigger multiple first-filter rejections before any thesis was built. Solvency, dilution, revenue growth, ROIC, valuation -- the filter runs before the story gets a hearing.

The narrative was accurate. The structure was a trap. The system's job is to separate the two before capital is deployed -- not after five years of compounding tells you the answer.

This is a diagnostic illustration, not a recommendation on ARKG or any holding within it.
