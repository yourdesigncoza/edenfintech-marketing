More research into a value trap thesis does not reduce the trap risk. It amplifies it.

Every institutional investor has access to the same 10-K filings, earnings calls, and analyst reports. The reason sophisticated investors still walk into value traps is not information asymmetry. It is the cognitive architecture of the analyst themselves. The value trap problem is a self-deception problem, not an information problem.

This means a solution that addresses information scarcity -- more data sources, better models, longer hours -- cannot fix it. The failure mode is in the reasoning process, not in the raw inputs. The analyst who does the most work on a bad thesis arrives at the most confident wrong conclusion.

"I have a rigorous process with multiple review layers." Most investment committees do. But review layers staffed by people who can see the analyst's score and price target are epistemically compromised before they begin. They validate the number rather than challenge the thesis.

EdenFinTech's response is to treat honesty as an architectural requirement, not a professional norm. The Adversarial Validator and Blind Epistemic Reviewer are not people asked to be critical -- they are pipeline components that are structurally prevented from doing anything else. The reviewer cannot retrieve the analyst's score. The blackout is enforced in code, not policy.

The problem is not your research. The problem is that your process is optimised to confirm what you already believe.
