Four out of ten "cheap" stocks will permanently destroy your capital.

That is the base rate. Academic research on the cheapest quintile of stocks consistently shows that 40-45% of statistically cheap equities are permanently impaired businesses, not mispriced opportunities waiting for a catalyst.

The asymmetry here works against you. The losing 40-45% do not just underperform -- they destroy capital through direct losses and elevated volatility drag that the surviving winners must overcome before generating any net return. You are not starting from zero. You are starting from a hole.

The natural response is "I do my own research -- I am not just buying everything that screens cheap." That is the wrong defence. The failure mode is not informational. It is cognitive. More research on a value trap thesis deepens conviction in the wrong direction. The analyst who does the most work on a bad thesis arrives at the most confident wrong conclusion.

The diagnostic challenge is separation before entry -- knowing which of the surviving 55-60% you are holding before a further 40% decline tells you.

At EdenFinTech, the first filter enforces this structurally: solvency, dilution, revenue growth, ROIC, valuation. Fail any single criterion and the investigation ends. No thesis is built. No conviction is formed. The filter runs before the analyst can fall in love with the story.

Separation before entry. Not conviction after losses.
