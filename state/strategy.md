**Carousel rules (backed by data):**

- Text-oriented carousels outperform image-oriented carousels.
- Story arc: Hook slide → Problem → Mechanism → Evidence → Conclusion → CTA.
- Keep captions under 100 characters per slide.

## 3. Hook Type Performance

Based on Algorithm Insights 2024 Edition, Buffer good-hooks research (2025), PressRocket.ai analysis, and finance-specific hook data.

Strong opening line boosts reader retention by up to 30% (Algorithm Insights 2024). Hooks under 12 words perform 20% better. First 210-235 characters are visible before "See More" — the hook must land within this window.

### Hook Types Ranked by Fit for EdenFinTech

| Hook Type | Why It Works | Fit for EdenFinTech | Priority |
|-----------|-------------|---------------------|----------|
| **Counterintuitive claim / Pattern interrupt** | Breaks assumptions, forces re-reading, highest stop-scroll rate. Example: "You don't have an information edge. You have a self-deception problem." | Perfect alignment with SKILL.md core thesis — the enemy is not bad data, it is the analyst's own reasoning. | **Highest** |
| **Named failure mode (problem-first)** | Names a specific pain point before offering any solution. Highest resonance with analytical professionals who self-identify with the problem. Required by pipeline/rules.md. | Native to all 5 pillars. RIAs and sophisticated investors are acutely aware of value trap risk — naming it precisely is the hook. | **Highest** |
| **Curiosity gap with quantified promise** | Tease specific data without revealing the conclusion. Finance audience responds strongly to number-anchored hooks. Example: "Three conditions must all be true before the scanner will even open a file. Most stocks fail the first one." | High — pairs naturally with mechanism and asymmetry math pillars. | **High** |
| **Specific mechanism reveal** | Describes one precise system behaviour in plain language. Developer audience responds strongly. | High for developer audience and system architecture pillar. | **High** |
| **Pre-mortem / kill-path** | Leads with the thesis rejection outcome, traces the reasoning backward. | Strong for case study and adversarial architecture pillars. Rare on LinkedIn = strong pattern interrupt. | **High** |
| **Direct question to reader's assumption** | Challenges a belief the reader holds ("Are you sizing positions based on conviction language?"). | Moderate — use sparingly, every 4-5 posts to drive comment engagement. | **Medium** |
| **Story / personal result** | Authentic story with outcome. | Low-medium — EdenFinTech tone is peer-to-peer analytical, not personal narrative. Reserve for milestone posts. | **Low** |

**Default hook type:** Counterintuitive claim or named failure mode. Per pipeline/rules.md: every hook MUST lead with a failure mode or problem.

## 4. Optimal Posting Schedule

### Financial Services — Specific Data (from Sprout Social, Hootsuite, LinkIntel, Buffer 2025-2026)

**Primary windows:**
- Wednesday 8:00-10:00 AM local time — highest single-day engagement for financial services; aligns with pre-market professional browsing and "thought leadership" moment mid-week
- Thursday 11:00 AM – 1:00 PM — strong secondary window; decision-makers between meetings
- Tuesday 7:45-9:00 AM — pre-market routine for finance professionals; LinkIntel: +26% above average

**Finance-specific insight:** Finance professionals check LinkedIn before market open (7-9 AM EST) and after market close (5-6 PM EST). Avoid posting between 12-3 PM as market monitoring absorbs attention. (LinkIntel, hashmeta.com, 2025)

**Buffer 2026 update (4.8M posts):** Late afternoon/evening (3-8 PM) now showing strong engagement across all professional content — a shift from prior morning-only dominance. Wednesday 4 PM, Friday 3-4 PM are top performers overall. Test the post-market window for EdenFinTech given finance audience behaviour.

**Posting frequency:**
- **Start:** 3 posts per week (Tue, Wed, Thu)
- **Target:** 4-5 posts per week once content pipeline is stable
- **Maximum:** Once per day — more than daily fragments impressions
- **Minimum for algorithm recognition:** 2 posts per week consistently delivers 5.6x more follower growth than irregular posting (HubSpot via ThinkPod, 2025)

**First-hour engagement rule:** Algorithm evaluates posts heavily in the first 60 minutes. Author must be available to respond to comments in the first hour after posting. Posts with active author engagement within 3 hours achieve 45% higher total reach (LinkIntel, 2025).

## 5. Post Length Recommendations

Finance/consulting optimal: 1,000-1,400 characters for standard text posts. Data-backed posts perform 52% better (ciela.ai, 2025). B2B decision-makers are time-constrained — senior financial professionals have high engagement when content directly impacts their work but very limited time.

| Content Goal | Recommended Length | Rationale |
|-------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Hook-only reframe (text only) | 300-700 characters | Quick pattern interrupt; high comment rate |
| Mechanism explainer | 1,000-1,400 characters | Finance audience optimal per ciela.ai data |
| Diagnostic framework | 1,200-1,600 characters | Medium-form: 5.8% avg engagement rate; requires "See More" click (40% do) |
| Case study / pre-mortem | 1,200-1,600 characters | Story arc requires space; saves and shares optimised at 1,000-1,300 characters |
| Thought leadership | 1,400-2,000 characters | Only when established authority warrants it; completion rate drops sharply after 1,500 characters |
| Carousel per-slide | 100-200 characters | Maximum per slide for mobile readability |

**Hook zone:** First 210-235 characters are visible without "See More" click. The failure mode or problem must be fully named within this zone.

**Content mix target:** 70% medium-length posts (300-700 words / 1,000-1,600 chars), 20% short-form (<300 words), 10% long-form (800+ words). (autoposting.ai, 2025)

## 6. Audience Split and Engagement Patterns

### Investor Audience (RIAs, wealth managers, sophisticated investors)

**What they engage with:**
- Principles-based and philosophy posts — these are 1% of published wealth management content but generate the highest engagement rate (Kitces/Sprout study, 2024)
- Original analytical frameworks, not commentary on news (news commentary underperforms among this group)
- Named failure modes they recognise from their own practice
- Data-backed claims with specific source citations (not "studies suggest")
- Methodology transparency — seeing rejections as often as passes builds trust before a track record exists

**What they avoid or ignore:**
- Promotional tone / brand-to-retail language
- Generic market commentary without analytical edge
- Unqualified return claims
- AI hype language ("AI-powered" as headline)

**Content mix for investor audience:**
- 40% educational (diagnostic frameworks, asymmetry math)
- 25% thought leadership (adversarial architecture, self-deception as design problem)
- 20% case studies (system output — passes and rejections)
- 10% market commentary (only when tied to a specific analytical mechanism)
- 5% system transparency (behind-the-scanner moments)

**Hook approach:** Lead with the investor's own failure mode. "The value trap problem is not an information problem" is an example of naming the failure mode they carry. Do not lead with the system's features.

### Developer Audience (quant devs, fintech builders, LLM engineers)

**What they engage with:**
- Specific technical architecture — pipeline stages, validation mechanisms, deterministic overrides
- LLM hardening and adversarial prompting patterns (genuinely rare topic on LinkedIn)
- Concrete system behaviours described in plain language with mechanism specifics
- Named design decisions with stated tradeoffs (why a Blind Epistemic Reviewer cannot see analyst scores — the enforcement is in code, not policy)
- Counterintuitive architecture choices that challenge conventional LLM pipeline wisdom

**What they avoid:**
- Surface-level "AI in finance" content (oversaturated, low credibility signal)
- Marketing language applied to technical content
- Claims without implementable specifics

**Hook approach:** Lead with the technical failure mode EdenFinTech was designed to prevent. "LLM narratives override quantitative red flags" is a developer-resonant problem that the adversarial pipeline directly addresses.

**Audience split target:** 60% investor / 40% developer across the full content calendar. Adjust based on engagement data after first 6 posts.

## 7. Hashtag Strategy

LinkedIn's 2024 algorithm update removed hashtag following, eliminated them from profile displays, and stopped showing them in dropdown search. Hashtags now function as SEO amplifiers within keyword-driven content, not discovery tools. Strategic hashtag use still improves targeted reach when combined with keyword optimisation. (Sprout Social, Oct 2025)

**Rules:**
- Use 3-5 hashtags per post maximum. Fewer is better. Do not exceed 5.
- Place hashtags at the end of the post, never inline.
- Mix 1-2 broad hashtags with 2-3 niche-specific ones.
- Rotate combinations to test effectiveness.

**Recommended hashtag sets by pillar:**

| Pillar | Primary (broad, high-follower) | Niche (targeted) |
|--------|-------------------------------|------------------|
| Diagnostic | #ValueInvesting, #Investing | #DeepValue, #ValueTrap |
| Adversarial Architecture | #Fintech, #AI | #LLMPipeline, #AdversarialAI |

| Case Studies | #Investing, #StockMarket | #TurnaroundInvesting, #DeepValue |

**Avoid:** #FinancialFreedom, #MassiveGains, #PassiveIncome — misaligned with peer-to-peer analytical tone and attract wrong audience.

**Hashtags to use consistently across all posts (brand anchors):**

## 9. CTA Strategy

**CTA type by goal:**

| Goal | CTA Type | Placement |
|------|----------|-----------|
| Engagement / follower growth | Question CTA ("What metric do you use to separate solvency scare from permanent impairment?") | End of post |
| Authority / saves | No CTA or implicit ("save this for the next time you're reviewing a beaten-down name") | End of post |
| Lead generation | Service link with value frame ("The scanner report format is described in the link in comments") | Comment pinned, not post body — avoids link reach penalty |
| Developer collaboration | Technical reference ("The pipeline stage described above is in the public architecture docs") | End of post |

**Key rule:** LinkedIn penalises posts with external links in the post body. For any CTA requiring a URL, place the link in the first comment, then reference the comment in the post body ("link in comments").

**CTA frequency:** Not every post needs a CTA. Authority-building posts (mechanism explainers, pre-mortems, frameworks) perform better without hard CTAs. Reserve explicit lead-gen CTAs for every 3rd-4th post.

## 10. Active Directives

These are standing instructions for all briefs and drafts until overridden by performance data.

1. **Default hook type:** Counterintuitive claim or named failure mode. Never lead with a feature.
2. **Default format:** Carousel for frameworks and mechanisms. Text + Image for reframes.
3. **Carousel slide count:** 6-9 slides. Monitor slide click-through — if below 35%, shorten.
4. **Pillar priority order this cycle:** Diagnostic > Adversarial Architecture = Asymmetry Math > Case Studies > System Architecture.
5. **Audience targeting default:** Investor audience unless the insight is technically specific to pipeline design.
6. **Posting frequency:** 3x/week (Tue/Wed/Thu) until backlog supports 4-5x.
7. **Post timing:** Tuesday 7:45-9:00 AM, Wednesday 8:00-10:00 AM, Thursday 11:00 AM – 1:00 PM. Test Wednesday 4:00 PM as secondary slot.
8. **Length default:** 1,000-1,400 characters for text posts. Hook must be complete within first 210 characters.
9. **Rejections:** Show at least 1 rejection walkthrough per 5 posts published. This is the pre-launch track record substitute.
10. **Hashtags:** 3-5 per post, end of post only. Always include #DeepValue and #ValueInvesting. Add 1-2 pillar-specific niche hashtags.
11. **External links:** Place in first comment, reference in post body. Never embed in post body.
12. **CTA cadence:** Explicit lead-gen CTA on every 3rd-4th post only.
